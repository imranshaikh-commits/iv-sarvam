# Meet Sarvam

*A one-page introduction for the Inspirit Vision delivery team.*

---

## Who is Sarvam?

**Sarvam** (सर्वम्) is Sanskrit for *"all, everything, the whole"* — and that's precisely his job. He's IV's in-house **Proposal Architect**, built to help us turn RFPs into polished, client-ready proposals in a fraction of the time it currently takes.

He is not a chatbot. He is not a search engine. Think of him as a very well-read junior consultant who has read every proposal IV has ever sent, remembers all of them, and works alongside you to draft the next one.

---

## What Sarvam does

- **Interviews you** about a new deal — client, industry, vendor, scale, key integrations.
- **Proposes an architecture** based on the closest matching past IV deployment. You approve, reject with comments, or ask for revisions.
- **Drafts the full proposal** section by section — Company Profile, Similar Experience, Understanding of Scope, Solution Architecture, Implementation Approach, RACI, Timeline, Commercials structure.
- **Compresses the output** so what used to be a 20MB DOCX is now a 3–5MB one that actually sends over email.
- **Learns from every won proposal** — approved final versions go back into his memory so the next deal starts smarter.

---

## What Sarvam won't do

- **He won't invent client references or metrics.** If he doesn't know something, he says so.
- **He won't fill in pricing.** Commercials are always your call. He'll set up the table; you and Ashish fill in the numbers.
- **He won't start drafting until you approve the architecture.** This is a hard gate — no shortcuts.
- **He won't be sycophantic.** No "Great question!", no exclamation marks, no 🎉 emojis. He talks like a senior consultant, not a marketing intern.

---

## How to work with Sarvam

**Opening a session:**
Just tell him the client and deal type.
> *"New deal — DFCC Bank in Sri Lanka, IAM refresh, likely SailPoint."*

**During discovery:**
He'll ask one focused question at a time. Answer conversationally — he'll follow up naturally.

**At the architecture gate:**
He'll show you a Mermaid diagram in the chat. If you don't like it, tell him exactly what to change:
> *"Drop the DR site, swap the LB for F5, add a second Zoho HR integration."*

He'll regenerate. Approve when it's right. **Nothing else happens until you approve.**

**During drafting:**
Sit back — he'll work section by section, escalating pricing/compliance language to a stronger model automatically.

**When it's done:**
He'll hand over two files (Lite ~3–5MB, Full ~10–15MB) and a short summary of what needs your review before it ships.

---

## When to override Sarvam

Sarvam is very good at 80% of the work. The other 20% is yours:

1. **Anything numeric that hits the client's wallet** — pricing tables, licence counts, effort estimates.
2. **New client references** — if Sarvam suggests naming a client, verify they've given you permission for that specific deal.
3. **Regulated-vertical compliance language** — banks, healthcare, government. Sarvam will draft, but the legal-safe version is still on us.
4. **Tone shift** — if he sounds too formal (or not formal enough) for a specific client, tell him. He adjusts.

---

## Behind the scenes

- Sarvam runs on our own AWS infrastructure — no client data ever leaves IV's control.
- His memory is our 100+ historical proposals, tagged and searchable by vendor, industry, deal size, and outcome.
- His LLM routing uses DeepSeek V4 Flash for speed, Claude Sonnet 4.6 for compliance-sensitive sections, and GLM 5.2 as backup.
- Everything he generates is logged and auditable — we can always trace where a claim came from.

## FAQ

**Q: What if Sarvam gets it wrong?**
Tell him. Rejection with specific comments is the loop he's designed for. He improves within a session and across sessions.

**Q: Can I use Sarvam for a proposal that's very different from anything we've done?**
Yes — but he'll flag low confidence and draft from a template rather than force weak retrieval. That's a feature, not a bug.

**Q: Does he replace me?**
No. He replaces the boring, repetitive 60% of proposal work (assembling boilerplate, formatting, sizing tables) so you can spend your time on the 40% that actually wins deals — commercials, client relationship insight, and technical judgement.

**Q: What if I want him to sound different?**
His voice is deliberately calibrated. If you have strong feedback on tone, raise it with Imran — we'll adjust the persona spec formally, not per-conversation.
